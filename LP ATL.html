Learning Engagement: Learner Profile Connections

How Could the Learner Profile Enhance What We Already Do?

Part 2: Collaborative Design Activity

This exercise will help your table group explore the dynamic connections between the **IB Learner Profile attributes**, **Approaches to Learning (ATL) skills**, and **subject areas**.

1.

Choose Your Focus

Decide on one core element to place at the center of your team's **web or mind map**:

  • **Option A:** A single **IB Learner Profile attribute** (e.g., *Inquirer*).
  • **Option B:** A single **subject area** (e.g., *Science* or *Art*).
2.

Define Context

Choose a specific age or class context for your learners (e.g., *Grade 7*, *Kindergarten*, *DP History*).

3.

Brainstorm & Map

Use the chart paper provided to develop your map. Your focus determines the direction:

  • **If Option A was chosen (Attribute):** Brainstorm ways learners could develop this **single attribute** within various learning experiences, and link relevant **ATL Skills**.
  • **If Option B was chosen (Subject):** Brainstorm examples of learning experiences within this subject area that would develop a **variety of Learner Profile attributes**, and link relevant **ATL Skills**.
4.

Finalize & Share

Complete your mind map or web with your team's ideas, and prepare to share your key insights and design concepts with the whole group.

Approaches to Learning (ATL) Skills

These skills help learners become self-directed, balanced, and reflective.

  • Thinking Skills

    Critical thinking, creativity, transfer of knowledge, problem-solving.

  • Communication Skills

    Listening, speaking, reading, writing, presenting ideas clearly.

  • Social Skills

    Collaboration, empathy, leadership, resolving conflict.

  • Self-Management Skills

    Organisation, reflection, emotional control, perseverance.

  • Research Skills

    Finding, analysing, evaluating, and using information effectively.
